How Does a Working Capital Loan Work?

A working capital loan helps businesses manage daily operational expenses like salary payments, inventory purchases, supplier payments, and cash flow gaps. Simply apply online, submit your business details and documents, and lenders check your working capital loan eligibility based on your business profile and repayment capacity. Once approved, the loan amount is transferred to your bank account, and repayment is made through easy monthly EMIs.

The process is fast and mostly digital—submit your details, complete documentation, get eligibility checked, and receive quick approval with direct fund disbursal into your business account.

WORKING CAPITAL OPTIONS

Types of Working Capital Loans

Explore different working capital financing options designed to help businesses manage cash flow, operations, and short-term funding requirements.

01

Term Loan for Working Capital

A short-term funding option that provides a lump sum amount for operational expenses with structured repayment over a fixed tenure.

02

Working Capital Demand Loan (WCDL)

Flexible short-term financing that allows businesses to access funds as needed for urgent working capital requirements.

03

Business Overdraft

Helps businesses withdraw funds beyond their available account balance up to an approved limit for short-term expenses.

04

Invoice Financing

Unlock funds against unpaid invoices to maintain cash flow and manage business operations without waiting for customer payments.

05

Cash Credit Facility

A revolving credit facility that allows businesses to borrow funds for working capital needs based on approved limits.

06

Merchant Cash Advance

Ideal for businesses with card sales, offering quick access to funds repaid through future business receivables.

A Working Capital Loan is a type of business loan that helps you meet day-to-day operational expenses — like inventory, salaries, rent, and supplier payments — and manage short-term cash flow gaps without disturbing your business funds.

Yes. A Working Capital Loan is offered as a category of business loan. While a regular business loan is often used for expansion or large investments, a working capital loan specifically funds short-term, day-to-day operational needs.

Business owners and enterprises in manufacturing, services, and trading sectors can apply. Most lenders prefer applicants aged 21 to 65 years, with a business vintage of around 2 years and a healthy annual turnover.

Many working capital loans are unsecured and need no collateral, especially for businesses with a good credit history. Secured options are also available against property or other assets, usually at lower interest rates and for higher amounts.

Common types include short-term loans, cash credit, overdraft facility, line of credit, invoice/bill discounting, and trade finance — each suited to different short-term financing needs.

Typically KYC documents (PAN, Aadhaar), business proof, GST returns, ITR, P&L and balance sheet of the last 2–3 years, and bank statements for the last 12 months. Secured loans may also need property or asset documents.

It depends on the type. For term loans, interest is charged on the full amount. For overdraft, cash credit, or line of credit facilities, you pay interest only on the amount you actually use, not the entire sanctioned limit.

A CIBIL score of 700+ is generally preferred, and 750+ helps you access lower interest rates. A strong business profile and steady revenue further improve your chances and terms.

With complete documentation, working capital loans — especially collateral-free ones — are often approved and disbursed within a few hours to 48 hours, depending on the lender and your profile.
