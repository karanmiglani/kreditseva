A credit card allows you to make purchases using a pre-approved credit limit provided by the issuing bank. You can repay the used amount within the billing cycle or convert eligible purchases into EMIs.

Common documents include PAN card, Aadhaar card, address proof, salary slips or ITR, bank statements, and valid contact details.

Eligibility depends on employment type, income, age, credit profile, and the financial institution's approval criteria.

KreditSeva helps users compare suitable credit card options and supports them through the application journey.

Processing timelines vary depending on profile verification, document checks, and the issuing institution's approval workflow.

A credit limit is the maximum amount you can spend using your credit card. It is determined by the issuing bank based on your income, credit score, repayment history, and overall financial profile.

Missing a payment may result in late payment fees, interest charges on the outstanding amount, and a negative impact on your credit score. It is advisable to pay at least the minimum due amount before the due date.

Yes, most credit cards allow you to convert eligible purchases into easy monthly instalments (EMIs). The availability, interest rate, and tenure of EMI options depend on the issuing bank's terms and the merchant.

A debit card deducts money directly from your bank account, while a credit card lets you borrow funds up to a pre-approved limit which you repay later. Credit cards also offer rewards, cashback, and purchase protection benefits that debit cards typically do not.

Each credit card application triggers a hard inquiry on your credit report, which may cause a minor temporary dip in your credit score. However, maintaining a good repayment history after approval can positively impact your score over time.

Credit cards can offer a variety of benefits including reward points, cashback, airport lounge access, travel miles, fuel surcharge waivers, dining discounts, shopping offers, and insurance coverage — depending on the card type and issuing bank.
