REPAYMENT OPTIONS

Types of Business Loan Repayment

Choose the repayment structure that fits your business cash flow best.

01

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment)

EMI is the most common business loan repayment option, where you pay a fixed amount every month that includes both principal and interest. This makes repayment predictable and easier for businesses to manage monthly cash flow without financial stress.

02

Loan Foreclosure

Foreclosure allows you to repay the entire outstanding business loan amount before the loan tenure ends. If your business receives extra income or surplus cash, closing the loan early can help you save significantly on interest costs. Some lenders may apply foreclosure terms or a lock-in period.

03

Part Prepayment

Part prepayment gives you the flexibility to repay a portion of your business loan before the scheduled tenure. This helps reduce your outstanding principal and lowers the overall interest burden. It's a smart option for businesses that occasionally generate extra cash and want to become debt-free faster.

04

Bullet Repayment

In a bullet repayment plan, you pay only the interest during the loan tenure, while the full principal amount is paid in one lump sum at the end. This option works well for businesses expecting a large payment or future revenue inflow, offering better short-term cash flow flexibility.
